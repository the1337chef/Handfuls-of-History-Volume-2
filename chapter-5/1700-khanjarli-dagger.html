Khanjarli

Circa 1700

The khanjarli is one a several types of exotic Indian daggers. The khanjarli is primarily associated with the Hindu states, many of which by the 1600s were clients of the Mughal Empire. The khanjarli is characterized by a strongly re-curving blade and a wide lunette-shaped pommel. This is an excellent early example, probably early 18th century or earlier. The nine-inch-long blade is forged from very good wootz (true Damascus) watered steel. The lunette shaped pommel and grips are ivory. It would have been made for a very well-off member of the Kshatriya caste or perhaps given as a gift by a sovereign to some favored member of his court. Although it is not particularly ostentatious, the elegance and beauty of this piece is obvious. Whether it would have been a practical knife is another question. It certainly could have been used to deal a deadly blow and the thickened tip is designed to be able to pierce armor. This would have been a dagger to wear at court, fitting for the fashions of the time and place. All royal courts have intrigues, and this knife would have been useful to have, whether called upon to defend or be used to attack a rival. It is not, however, a knife that I imagine would have been useful at the table.
