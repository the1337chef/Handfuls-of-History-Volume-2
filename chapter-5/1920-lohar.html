Circa 1920

Weapons take many forms and battleaxes became popular in Europe and elsewhere for their armor piercing potential. This pick/ax is a lohar, which is a traditional weapon for men of the Banochie (Banuchi) tribe in the Khyber Pass area between Pakistan and Afghanistan. These tribesmen have always been a great thorn in the side of any would-be conqueror of the region, from Alexander the Great, to the Europeans and Americans of recent centuries. Each man of the tribe makes and decorates his own Lohar, so that each is quite unique. Many are made so the blade will fold into the handle, although the blade is fixed on this example. It has been suggested that the fixed blade form is an older style and that folding style is perhaps made for sale to tourists. The design elements, engravings, and inlays on this piece are fairly typical.

The Khyber is an area ripe for ambushes and this sort of weapon would be deadly indeed in close quarters, and perhaps required more skill to use than a sword.
