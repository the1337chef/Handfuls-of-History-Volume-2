Circa 1760

The Yali is a Hindu demon often incorporated into hilts of swords or daggers. This dagger from South India is close in shape to a bichwa, but does not have the usual bichwa loop style grip. The handle on this piece is very small. It is only comfortable for me to hold in my whole hand, ignoring the knuckle guard. Perhaps it was intended for a woman. Stylistically, it is similar to an Indian (Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu) bichuwa dagger in the Metropolitan Museum (Bequest of George C. Stone, 1935), and almost identical to it in dimensions. The total length is 10.5 inches with a 7.5-inch blade. The blade on this piece is heavily patinated and so it is not possible to tell whether or not it is wootz steel.
