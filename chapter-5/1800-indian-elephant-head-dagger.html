An eBay search on the term "wootz," the crucible steel discovered in medieval India, brought me to this interesting little item. The blade does appear to have the "water steel" appearance of wootz, though not of particularly high quality or contrast as the steel of other pieces like the tiger tooth dagger. The shape of the blade is also rather ordinary, so the real curiosity is the handle, which is likely far newer than the blade. I think of this as a piece of folk art more than as a cultural item. It appears to be carved bone, perhaps somehow stained to give it the appearance of more age than it probably deserves. The carving appears to be a small lion held captive by an elephant. The lion does not seem to be in any particular distress, although it is being held upside down. The entire creation may have been to salvage an old blade and make it marketable as a tourist item. In the modern world that took it to the internet. It was not expensive for a wootz blade so here it is, whatever it is.
