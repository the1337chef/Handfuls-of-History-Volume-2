Circa 1880

The pichangeti is the heirloom dagger of the Coorg people of Southwest India. They are passed on though generations in Coorg families and seldom become available for sale. For more about the Coorg people see the entry on the ayda katti, their traditional battle sword. The Coorg people are thought to predate the arrival of the Aryans into India and the Coorg forms of Hindu worship blend traditions from their tribal past and more recent history. However, the Coorg are by no means a primitive people. They have been important participants in modern Indian history, but are also determined to preserve aspects of their cultural distinctness. The Coorg traditionally take pride in their warrior prowess, and weapons, including firearms, are highly significant symbols for them, even in their religious observances. The pichangeti and ayda katti are both emblematic for them. They no longer carry swords but a pichangeti would be an expected accessory for an upper class Coorg man in his formal native costume. While the ayda katti really is a fearsome weapon, the pichangeti is mostly symbolic and decorative. The edge is not even sharp on this example. As on many pichangeti, stylized representations of ayda katti are engraved on the gilded scabbard.
