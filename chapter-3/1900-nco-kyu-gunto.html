Circa 1900

This kyu guntō comes from the early years of Japan's military expansion in the twentieth century. It is a variation on a style known as the Type 19, first introduced in 1886. It is strictly in the style of European sabers of the time and would not have been a very functional weapon but rather carried more as a mark of rank. However, the quality of the piece would not suggest a particularly high status for the owner. Most likely it belonged to a non-commissioned officer, or perhaps a lieutenant. The blade is machine-made, probably plated and the temper line is totally fake, not something produced in the forging process. It would date to time of the Sino-Japanese (1894-1895) or perhaps the Russo-Japanese war (1905). There is no mon (family crest) and so a soldier with a common background would have used it, typical for that era. You will often see swords of this type misidentified as World-war II era and priced much higher than they should be.
