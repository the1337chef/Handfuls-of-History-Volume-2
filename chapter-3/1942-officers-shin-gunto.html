Officer's Shin Guntō (Type 98)

Circa1942

The Type 98, and the somewhat earlier Type 94 shin guntōs were the standard swords for Japanese officers in the early years of the war. They would be considered tachi, or slung swords, since the scabbards have loops allowing them to be carried on belt. The rank of the owner would have once been indicated by the color and other details of a tassel, which would have been mounted through the saru-te (tassel loop) on the pommel, both of which are missing on this example (a tassel loop is present on the Type 95 Shin Guntō). Otherwise, all of the standard fittings are present on this sword including the grip wrappings and cherry blossom menuki. The grip covering appears to be ray skin under the silk wrappings. In the opinion of my knife maker friend, Steve Bloom, it is a hand-forged blade. The temper line is faint and there are signs of poorly done polishing. It is signed by Hiromitsu.

There is a button on the base of the grip to lock the blade in the scabbard. There might have been a second hanger loop on the scabbard once, but these were made to be removable and were only common on the Type 94 models. There is a ding on the lower part of the scabbard that is possibly from a bullet.
