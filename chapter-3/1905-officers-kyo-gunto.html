Officer's Kyu Guntō

Circa 1905

This sword is an interesting blend of traditional Japanese style and that of the new military style that Japan was trying to invent for itself in the European mold. It is a high quality "kyu guntō" with an elongated grip that is somewhat reminiscent of the long two-handed grip of traditional katana. The quality of the piece makes it unlikely that it was strictly machine made, but the presence of the fuller, which has been filled in with red paint, makes it clear that this is not an older blade that was refit. The fitting is consistent with it coming from the period of the Russo-Japanese war (1905), but it certainly might have remained in service until the 1930s, when the Japanese sword regulations were revised. Although it is clearly an officer's sword, there is no mon (family crest) on the grip, so the officer was unlikely to have come from an old noble family but rather someone promoted through the military education system.
