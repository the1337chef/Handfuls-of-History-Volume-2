Billao "Dervish" Sword

View image full-size

Circa 1906

The national identity of Somali grew from a short-lived Islamic state that arose in the first decade of the 20th century and lasted only to 1920. This was the so-called Dervish state (not related to the Dervishes of the Ottoman world), which grew from the inspired leadership of Muhammad Abdullah Hassan (for more information see the entry on the Billao dagger).

This is a short sword developed from the Somali Billao belawa dagger and it shares some of the same distinctive features. The non-symmetrical leaf shape blade is about 60 percent longer than that of the dagger and the pommel is of the iconic three-pronged shape. The belt and scabbard are probably original and so may date to the time of the historic Dervish state.
