Circa 2000

This relatively recent-made piece was a gift from my friend Rodney Stubina. He acquired it when he was working for the U.S. government in Chad. The tribal people are called by various names: Tebu, Toubou, or Tubu (from Old Tebu, meaning "Rock People"). They constitute an ethnic group inhabiting northern Chad, southern Libya, northeastern Niger and northwestern Sudan. They are also sometimes known as the Gouran people.

It is a nice, simple knife with the handle and scabbard decorated with some sort of lizard skin. The stamps could be maker's marks, but more likely they have some type of talismanic intent. It was most likely essentially new when Rodney acquired it, but it is a traditional form and so might have been intended for native use. Where Rodney was working was definitely not a tourist area.
