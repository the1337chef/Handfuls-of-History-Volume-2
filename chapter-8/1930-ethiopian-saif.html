Circa 1930

This sword comes from the period when Haile Selassie, the Lion of Judea, ruled Ethiopia. The blade is European made, probably British, 31.5 inches long, finely etched with floral patterns, and with the "Lion of Judea" emblem of the emperor. There are initials on the reverse, C C or perhaps G C, which could be maker's marks. The handle of this example is made of three pieces of horn, as is traditional for Ethiopian swords. On other, somewhat later, examples of this type of sword the hilts are Bakelite plastic. The hilt is very similar to that of the older Ethiopian gurade.

Haile Selassie I was born July 23, 1892, as Tafari Makonnen Woldemikael. He claimed direct descent from Makeda, the Queen of Sheba, and King Solomon of ancient Israel. He came to be an important symbol of African political equality through times when the continent of Africa was largely dominated by European colonial imperialism. Until close to the time of his death and forced abdication in September 12, 1974, he ruled Ethiopia as King and Emperor, except for a period (1936-1942) when an Italian invasion forced him into exile. The most enduring Empire in World history ended with his death on August 27, 1975.

Haile Selassie also came to be the symbol for the "Pan Africanism" movement of Marcus Garvey in the 1930s and even to be viewed as a messiah and god incarnate by the followers of the Rastafari movement. Regarding his divinity, he had the following to say of the Rastafari in Jamaica, "Who am I to disturb their belief?"

Haile Selassie was coronated on November 2, 1930 and this sword is likely from the early period of his reign. While earlier traditional Ethiopian swords, like the military gurades and the tribal shotels, are strongly curved, this is more of a broad sword. Therefore, it is a bit odd that it has come to be known as a saif, since Arab saifs are typically curved sabers or shamshirs. For as impressive a sword as it may be, it comes from a time when national armed forces relied on firearms rather than swords. However, the sword is a fitting emblem for a truly ancient Empire.
