Circa 1950

The Shi people (Mashi) reside in the eastern region of the Democratic Republic of the Congo (Zaire), near the Tanzania border. Reportedly, this is an example of the typical sword carried by a Shi warrior. It is a unique type, with a straight, double-edge blade, hand forged with a pronounced central rib. The slender blade is 22 inches long. Both the hardwood handle and scabbard are densely bound with twisted brass and copper wires

There are a vast number of tribal groups living in the Democratic Republic of the Congo and relatively few are well studied or have a documented history. Neither the internet, nor my reference books, have been able to provide me very much information on the Shi. They are part of the Khoisan people cluster within the Sub-Saharan African affinity block and their current population is estimated to be about 1.36 million. It is claimed that at the present time 97 percent have been converted to Christianity.

This is a well-made sword that seems like it could serve effectively as a weapon. The attribution and approximate date come from Artzi at Oriental Arms. Posts on the Ethnographic weapons forum support the attribution to the Shi people and I think Artzi assigns the "mid-20th century" date just due to the excellent condition.
