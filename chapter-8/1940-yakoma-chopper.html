Circa 1940

This style of short sword is associated with the Yakoma people who live primarily in the Central African Republic, with a very small number living the Democratic Republic of the Congo (Zaire). The neighboring Sangi and Ngbandi tribes use similar weapons. The Yakoma are a Bantu people but speak a distinct dialect. Their traditional tribal life was that of hunter gathers with minimal agriculture. The roughly 160,000 Yakoma in the Central African Republic represent approximately 4 percent of the population. André-Dieudonné Kolingba, president of the CAR from 1979 to 1993, was a Yakoma.

The weapon has a heavy, very well-forged, 8.5-inch blade with a thick "ear" at the base. The wood handle is bound with brass strips. The total length is 13.5 inches. As you see in the pictures, this weapon appears neither particularly threatening nor practical, suggesting that it might have been more ceremonial or intended to represent status within the tribe.
