Circa 1930

This type of dagger is associated with the Beja people who reside along the Western shores of the Red Sea in Egypt, Sudan and Eritrea. The Beja are reputedly descended from ancient Egyptian mercenary warriors who were displaced by a Nubian invasion after the collapse of the New Kingdom. Although they were converted to Islam in the 15th century, the various Beja tribes fought on both sides in the Mahdist Wars (1881-1899), between British forces and the armies of the Muhammad Ahmad bin Abd Allah, who had proclaimed himself the "Mahdi" of all Islam.

The major tribes of the Beja are Hadendoa and Beni Amer and these daggers are sometime named for these tribes, but are known more commonly as "X daggers" after their peculiar black wood handles. This dagger, with a hooked blade is one of rarest versions. The dagger came with the original scabbard and wide carrying belt of tooled leather.

Most of the Beja are now pastoral nomads. They claim to be descendants of Noah's grandson, Cush (son of Ham). Their native language is called Bedawiya, although many are also fluent in Arabic or Tigre. They are ostensibly Muslim, however in reality, their beliefs are interwoven with folklore and superstitions.
