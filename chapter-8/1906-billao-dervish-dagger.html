Circa 1906

At the beginning of the 20th century, the continent of Africa had been largely divided into pieces by the European powers, in the form of colonies and spheres of influence. The Turks had largely lost North Africa to the French and English, with Italy looking for a piece. Countries that established colonies all along the coast were making inroads into the interior. Aside from Ethiopia, the only truly African nation was the short lived Dervish state in Somali (Somali: Dawlada Daraawiish, Arabic: دولة الدراويش).

Muhammad Abdullah Hassan, a Sunni religious leader raised an army that between 1901 and 1904 fought a series of success campaigns against Ethiopian, British and Italian forces that allow for his forces, known as the Dervish army, to establish an independent nation in central Somali. The Dervish state was officially recognized by the Triple Entente powers; Germany, Austro-Hungary, and the Ottoman Turks, who were soon to oppose the allies of World War I.

Hassan, who took the title Sayyid, exploited Britain's need to withdraw from Africa in the face of the approaching European war to consolidate his position, building more than a dozen fortresses and a walled capital city at Taleh. The Dervish state created by Hassan lasted only until 1920 when the British returned to their neglected interests in Africa. With the aid of aerial attacks, the British took Taleh and the Sayyid fled with the remnants of his army. Although they never regained power, Muhammad Abdullah Hassan and other writers and poets of his short-lived era established a literary tradition that created a Somali national identity. Hassan is still considered by many to be the father of modern Somali. His legacy inspired later leaders to resist European hegemony and ultimately establish the modern Somali state.

This dagger, also known as a belawa, is of a style especially associated with the Dervish state. The belawa blade is traditionally leaf shaped but non-symmetrical. The pommels are sometimes three pronged, as on the Billao sword, or have a single central silver cap as on this example. The blade on this example is 8.5 inches long and the hide scabbard is likely to be the original.
