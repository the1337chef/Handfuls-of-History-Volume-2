British Entrenching Tool

1944

This certainly more of a tool than a weapon, but if push came to shove in any but the closest quarters, this might be a better choice for hand-to-hand combat than something like the Model 1917 trench knife. The profile of the head is not so far from that of a medieval war axe and the bayonet at the other end could serve as a thrusting weapon, every bit as good as the trench knife but with a much longer reach.

For as much as this could be a weapon in extremis, this would be just the thing for digging a latrine, a daily rather than occasional need for soldiers in the field. This British military-marked tool comes apart to make it easy to carry. It would have originally had kaki sheaths and frogs to attach it to the rest of a soldier's kit. The bayonet shown attached would have already been part of the soldier's kit, since it is a standard spike-type bayonet for the British SMLE (Small Magazine Lee Enfield) rifle. The purpose for attaching the bayonet to the shaft of the entrenching tool was to let it be used as a probe for land mines. I think I would have preferred an electromagnetic detector.
