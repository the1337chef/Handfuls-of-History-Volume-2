Fairbairn-Sykes Fighting Knife

1943

William Ewart Fairbairn and Eric Anthony Sykes are credited with the design of this style of commando knife that was issued to various British units after the Normandy landing in 1944, most notably units of the SAS, Airborne Forces, and Commandos. It was also used by some American troops deployed for similar commando missions. The design is reminiscent of a medieval stiletto, being small and therefore nearly invisible until used to deal a blow. However, unlike a classic stiletto, the blade of the commando knife is double edged and deadly sharp making it useful for slashing as well as thrusting attacks. Fairbairn was strong advocate for the specifics of this knife's design and he described his reasoning and advice for using such a knife in his book Get Tough!

The knives were made by the Wilkinson Sword Company and other English companies. Over the years there were minor variations in grip and pommel design. The first batch was produced in January 1941. This is a third pattern knife for which some of the features, such as the ringed grip, were simplified to speed up production. The sheath was designed so that it could be strapped on to an arm or leg for stealth and to reduce encumbrances to the user.
