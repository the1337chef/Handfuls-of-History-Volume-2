1862

Prior to 1861, the total number of cutlasses in the hands of U.S. sailors was only 6,600. With the vast military scale-up required by the Civil War, many thousands more would be needed. It was also recognized that a weapon better than the stopgap 1841 Model cutlass was needed. The U.S. Model 1841 cutlass was little more than the old-style foot artillery sword with a knuckle guard. The new 1861 Model followed the pattern of the French 1831 Model cutlass, with a broad saber-like single-edged blade and a well-formed hand guard. The U.S. Models were slightly lighter than the French swords and had brass, rather than steel, hand guards.

The swords were made by Ames Company, then located in Chicopee, Massachusetts. The first lot of 3,600 cutlasses was delivered in 1861, followed by 16,400 more in 1862. A final shipment of 2,000 was made in 1864, accounting for a total of 22,000. The blades were dated and the brass hilts stamped with serial numbers. The serial numbers are in two parts, a larger number with the letter M and then a smaller number beneath. The larger number indicates a lot of one thousand swords and the smaller number the sequence within that series. The marks on this sword are 9M over 443, indicating a serial number of 9,443, consistent with the 1862 production date marked on the blade below the U.S.N. and the inspector's initials.

Although missing the grip cover, this sword is otherwise in beautiful condition for likely having seen three years of service during the Civil War and probably longer service after. There are several photos comparing it to the French sword on which it was based.
