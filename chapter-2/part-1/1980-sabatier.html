Sabatier Chef's Knife

1980

If the Laguiole is a knife for a French gentleman, and the Opinel for the "everyman," a Sabatier like this one is for the French chef. Shortly after we were first married, my wife won a drawing and the prize was a gift certificate for a local cookery store. She wisely choose this knife and it has been a favorite ever since.

Sabatier sounds like it should be like a famous brand name, but like Laguiole it is not a trade name for a single company. Also, like Laguiole, knives by this name originated in Thiers, France. Most knives sold by this name are of good quality with fully forged construction, so that blade, bolster, and full size tang are of a single piece of metal. However, some companies, even some based in France, will take advantage of the lack of trademark protection to market mass-produced inferior knives.

This knife has definitely proven to be one of the good ones. The blade is high carbon steel, as evidenced by its discoloration from over thirty-plus years of use. It has been plenty close to the stove too, as evidenced by the heat damage to the pommel. It pleases me to look at this knife and the medieval knife from Holland, see the similarities, and reflect of centuries of cuisine.
