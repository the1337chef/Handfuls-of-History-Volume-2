Chapter 2. Europe and the Americas

Part 1: American and European Non-Military Knives, Daggers, and Swords

There have probably been thousands of knives made for every sword that has ever been made through history, and of course that ratio is much higher today. We can define a knife broadly as a small instrument with a blade and a handle. Some knives, such as the Bowie type, are thought of as intended for fighting, but more often the intended purpose of a knife is to be used for dining or as a tool. However, even at that, the intended purpose of a steak knife is to cut flesh.

This very brief chapter cannot possibly do this enormous topic any real justice, especially on the subject of knives. Jack knives, other pocket knives, table knives, kitchen knives, work knives—the list could go on and on, and each type could provide information and examples for an entire book. Some people have large collections of knives from a single manufacturer, but I do not really collect “knives” at all. I just happen to have a couple of historically interesting knives that did not fit in any other chapter.
