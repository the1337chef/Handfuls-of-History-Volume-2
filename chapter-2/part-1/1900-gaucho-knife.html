Circa 1900

The frontiersman/ cowboy equivalents of the South American pampas were known as gauchos, and, as with the North American cowboy, the romantic image of the gaucho has outlived the pioneer days which created it. The origin of the term gaucho is uncertain: the Spanish word typically used to describe a cowboy is "vaquero." Gaucho may be derived from "chaucho," the Spanish term for a herdsman.

There are several styles of edged weapons associated with the gauchos. The facón and daga are large-bladed fighting knives, effectively short swords. Cuchilla are utilitarian knives of the gauchos, with full tangs and riveted wooden handles, resembling basic kitchen or butcher knives. These knives remain popular today and are of such basic design that only their national origin places them in the category of gaucho knives. Gaucho puñals, like this knife, combine a certain elegance with functionality. Usually they have decorative handles and blades better suited for the table than for a street fight. They often also had chased silver scabbards. In many cases these knives, or at least the blades, were of European (often German) manufacture, made for the South American market, just as many "Bowie knives" were made in England for the American market.

This knife is marked J.M. Mailhos, Montevideo. It is also marked "Acier fondu," which in French translates as cast steel. Montevideo is the capital and largest city of Uruguay. It is on the opposite shore of the Rio Uruguay bay from Buenos Ares, Argentina. The population of Montevideo is about 1.3 million, a third of the total population of the country. It is less than half the size of Buenos Ares.

The mythos of the Gaucho is shared by both Uruguay and Argentina. Although more commonly associated with Argentina, it may perhaps be of equal or greater significance to Uruguay. One of the gold plaques on the handle of this knife is the crest of Uruguay. Topped by the sun, the four quadrants of the circle (clockwise from upper right) are; a representation of the Montevideo hill fortress, an ox, a horse, and the scales of justice. The leather sheath is plain but nicely made, with a tab on the front side that would hold it in place if held under a belt.
