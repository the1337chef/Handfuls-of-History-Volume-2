Hungarian Fokos (Shepherd's) Axe

Circa 1890

The traditional axe carried by shepherds in the Carpathian mountains was probably derived from the weapons used by Hungarian warriors as far back as the 9th century. While a traditional shepherd's crook might be useful to prod sheep, it would not have had much use as a weapon. The Fokos axe on the other hand, could defend shepherd and flock alike from brigands and wild animals, or be used to trim tree branches and chop firewood. This is an older example, with a heavy steel head repurposed from a naval boarding axe. Its 34-inch long hardwood shaft is characteristically carved and painted. The shaft has a steel cap at the end to let it serve as a walking stick.

Modern versions of the Fokos axe are more decorative and seldom used as tools or weapons. The axe heads are often brass or even simply carved wood. They are used in traditional dances or sold to tourists as souvenirs.

The Polish folk hero Juraj Jánošík, was a local Robin Hood type character. A Fokos axe was to Jánošík what the bow and arrow was to Robin of Loxley.
