Circa 2000

For over 120 years the Opinel family has been selling knives that have become iconic for French culture. If Laguioles are the knives of the genteel, Opinels are for anyone and every day. They are simple, practical, and excellent tools. In France these knives are so common that the term Opinel has become a generic term for any wood-handled pocket knife.

The company is based in Saint-Jean-de-Maurienne, Savoy and sells approximately 15 million knives every year. The various models of Opinel differ in size and blade shape, some are specialized for particular purposes. We own one that is intended especially for cleaning mushrooms (though we don't use it much).

They usually have plain wooden handles and folding blades. The blades can locked in the open position by rotating the collar at the top of the handle. Not only do we have several Opinels in regular use, I find them such practical and affordable knives that I usually have a few extra new ones so that I can give one away to first time visitors to our home who are not familiar with these wonderful knives. One of the things that makes them so special is that you can still get them with high carbon steel blades. On this knife, "Lame acier au Carbone" translates to "Blade of carbon steel". The blades are thin but also strong and will take and hold a very sharp edge. They must be kept clean however, and even so, the blades darken, but no matter.

When I give away an Opinel I take my Dremel tool to the handle to make an appropriate inscription. The one pictured has been marked "garage" indicating that after being washed it is to be returned to the garage, which is where I entertain guests amongst the motorcycles, guns, goblets, and swords.
