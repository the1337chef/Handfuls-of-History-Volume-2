1940

In 1904 the Empire Knife Company, originally founded in 1889, moved to Franklinville, New York, where it was reorganized as the Ontario Knife Company. The founding president was Walter L. Burritt who died in 1923. He was replaced as president by James A. Chrestensen who, according to the company web site, introduced the "Old Hickory" line of kitchen knives. Other sources say the Old Hickory line goes back to the original company in 1889. In either case, basically the same knives are available for purchase today brand new. They are distinctive for having a decidedly old-fashioned appearance and still being made of high-carbon, non-stainless steel. This knife came from our utility knife block. We are not sure where we bought it or even if we bought it, rather than just came upon it somewhere. It's just a good old knife. The patterning in the blade keeps down the weight while adding strength. The "Old Hickory" brand is barely visible on the handle. Current versions of the knife still have the scimitar logo and the "Tru-Edge" stamp. They are $15 brand new.
