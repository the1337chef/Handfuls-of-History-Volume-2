Circa 1830

This high-quality, double-barreled percussion pistol was sold in Halifax England, quite likely by either the ironmonger Robert Wainhouse, or Edward Wainhouse, a merchant of Ward's End. Halifax is about halfway between London and the Scottish border, and by the latter half of the 19th century it was a growing industrial center.

There is a strange combination of elegance and deadly purposefulness about this pistol. If two shots should fail, a thumb catch releases a spring-loaded stiletto bayonet that automatically locks in place. It leads me to wonder what Halifax and the surrounding counties like in were 1835. Would a native of that area want a gun of elegant presence but such serious aggressive potential? Alternatively, was this combination weapon sold to a traveler who felt they would need the protection on the road or even at sea?

* This item also appears in Handfuls of History Volume 1 with more details about the Wainhouse family and Halifax.
