Circa 1750(?)

A left-hand dagger (main-gauche in French) was intended to be used as a parrying weapon in combination with a rapier. With a broad, up-turned guard, they were designed to catch and deflect a thrust. Their utility as a solo weapon would have been rather limited, except in quarters too tight to allow the use of the companion sword. Likewise, they were not blades that would have been practical in the kitchen or workshop. I have a hard time imagining someone using a main-gauche to cut a steak.

Things can get lost in history, and I can't say very much about this piece with confidence. It clearly is not a modern reproduction piece, nor is it strictly a costume piece. I purchased it at a Renaissance faire many years ago from a vendor who had other pieces with authentic age. It is solid and seems well made, but it is not a fancy piece. I would like to speculate that it is Italian, but really have no solid basis for that.
