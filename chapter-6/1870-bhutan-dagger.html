Circa 1870

Isolated at the edge of the Himalayas between India and Tibet, Bhutan is unique in never having been a conquered nation throughout its many thousand-year history. As in Tibet, Buddhism is an important civil as well as religious factor in Bhutan and for most of its history since 700 CE, Bhutan was ruled by both Lamas and kings or regional warlords. In the shadow of Tibet, Bhutan was both threatened and protected by its larger neighbor. However, in the late 1700s, when some of the secular leaders took interest in territorial expansion into Assam and Sikkim, then under British control, Bhutan attracted the attention of the English. The Bhutanese were compelled to grant concessions to the British and pull back to their original borders, but they also gained in Britain, and later independent India, new protectors and guardians of their isolationism. The current royal dynasty of Bhutan was established in 1907 by Ugyen Wangchuck with the blessings and support of the British. A succession of Wangchuck kings slowly moved Bhutan closer to being part of the modern world, and since 2005 Bhutan has been a formal constitutional monarchy.

Only in the last 20 years has Bhutan really been opened up to the modern world of television and the internet, and the Bhutanese are not anxious to embrace Western values. In 1972, Bhutan's fourth Dragon King, Jigme Singye Wangchuck declared that "Gross National Happiness is more important than Gross National Product," encouraging the people of his country to enjoy their spiritual values and splendid isolation. Bhutan has also urged the people of world to accept this philosophy, with relatively little success.

This dagger is in the classic style of Bhutan, although it may have originated in a neighboring part of Tibet. The cylindrical pommel is highly indicative of Bhutanese craftsmanship, and this is a particularly lovely example with a pierced brass setting, including small turquoise stones. The stiletto-style blade is 11 inches long and constructed of a form of "hair pin" folded pattern welded steel.
