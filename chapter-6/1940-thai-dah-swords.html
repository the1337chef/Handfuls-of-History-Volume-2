Circa 1940

Thailand was every much in the middle of things in Southeast Asian politics, but fortunately was recognized by both Great Britain and France as being in a spot to serve as buffer zone between the two European colonial empires.

The Thai people are primarily of Chinese descent but their culture has been shaped by many factors including the Hindu and Buddhist faiths from India. They also struggled to achieve independence from strong neighbors such as Burma to the west and the Empire of the Khmers to the east. The modern nation of Thailand was founded in 1769 by King Taksin the Great, who was succeed by Rama I, founder of the Chakri dynasty. During the era of Britain's Queen Victoria, the Thai king Mongkut, and subsequently his son Chulalongkorn, negotiated several treaties with the European powers that allowed the nation to retain its independence. The nation modernized more than most of its neighbors, and in 1903 Siam purchased half a million modern Mauser-type rifles made under license by the Tokyo arsenal in Japan.

Throughout the southeast, almost very man would own a dah sword, similar to those used in Burma. The style of these pieces suggests that they came from Thailand (Siam). It is likely that Vietnam War veterans brought both of these pieces to the U.S. as souvenirs. The smaller one is clearly the nicer of the two, but neither are particularly high quality, a fact that would not have strongly impacted their functionality.
