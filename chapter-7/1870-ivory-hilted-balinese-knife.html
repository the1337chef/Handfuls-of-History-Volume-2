Circa 1870

Bali is a land of exotic faces, both on the people and in the artwork that surrounds you as you walk through the villages. Statues flank the entrance to every family compound; some are fanciful, some are stylized, others are quite lifelike. The images on this little knife remind me of Bali and my one-time tour guide, Alit Krisnu, is convinced this knife is from Bali. However, this knife is really a bit of a puzzle since it is not recognizable as any common Indonesian form. It seems to blend features from multiple locales and the large face on the sheath is particularly unusual. It is also rather hard to place the features of the carved ivory figure. Are they really Indonesian or is there a blend of African or Chinese features as well?

Over all, it is a very nicely made piece, perhaps a unique custom item. The five-inch-long blade is made of pattern steel, but not quite typical Indonesian Pamor. Could the ivory figure be intended as a portrait of the owner, or perhaps an ancestor?
