Circa 1935

The tribal people of the mountainous region of Luzon in the Philippines are collectively known as the Igorot, derived from the archaic term for "mountain people" in the native language of the Philippine Tagalog people. The Bontoc, Ibaloi, Kalanguya, Ifugao, Isneg, Kalinga, and Kankanaey are all considered Igorot people. This particular style of knife, known as hinalung, is most often associated with the Ifugao. The blade is spear shaped and in fact has a hollow handle that would allow the blade to be mounted on a suitable staff and used as a spear for hunting, or perhaps historically for warfare. The blade is carried in an open wood scabbard called a hikot, bound with Braided rattan strips. The Igorot also use a type of knife with single-edged angled "bolo" blade, known as a pinahig that has similar handle usually covered with braided rattan strips. However, the pinahig handle is solid, since the blade is not suitable for use as a spearhead.

The hikot type scabbard is similar to that of the dao sword of the Naga, being open on one side. I speculate that this sort of scabbard has the advantage of not trapping water, as well as keeping the blade visible for inspection if cleaning is needed.
