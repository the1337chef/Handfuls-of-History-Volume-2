Circa 1890

The alamang is a sacred sword or cutlass of the Bugis and Makassarese people in Sulawesi. The Bugis villages touched on the dense forest of their islands and the Bugis were renown for turning their forest resources into serviceable vessels that plied the sealanes among the islands, either for trade or piracy as opportunities allowed. The heavy blade of native pamor steel is single edged. In the pommel can be seen an image of the hornbill, a sacred creature to many of the island tribes. While there are keris attributed to the Bugis, the alamang is more uniquely associated with them and would be much more robust weapon for the hand of a "Boogieman" pirate.
