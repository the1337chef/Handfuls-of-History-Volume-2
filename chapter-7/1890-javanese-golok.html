1890

The term golok is applied to many types of swords and machetes. This piece is similar in some ways to other swords known as pedangs, such as the pedang lurus, pedang also just being a term meaning "sword." With its thin straight blade, this style of golok is quite different from the silver mounted golok simpenan. However, like the simpenan type, this is not a blade for field or forest use. It is a very elegant and attractive weapon.

This particular style of golok is usually attributed to West Java, and some examples have the name of the village Tjikeroe, engraved on the blade. A common feature to this type of golok is the carved animal head on the hilt. Sometimes the animals resemble birds, other times they are more like lions or dogs. The hilt on this sword is to me most suggestive of a dog, but could just as easily be a stylized dragon.
