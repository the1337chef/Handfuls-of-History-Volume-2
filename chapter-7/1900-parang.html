Circa 1900

Parang is a generic term for a sword or machete that can be applied to weapons from anywhere in the archipelago. Lacking many distinctive features, it is as good a term as any to apply to this long knife that was acquired without any provenance. Likewise, the significance of the mark struck on the base of the blade is unknown. They appear close to the Latin letter TA or IA but not quite. It is most likely some sort of maker's mark. The blade appears to be ordinary high-carbon steel and the well-fitting simple leather scabbard is probably original to the knife. The shape of the blade resembles that of a dahong palay, a term which translates as "rice leaf," but could also refer to a green snake indigenous to the island of Luzon in the Philippines, which is the likely source of this knife.
