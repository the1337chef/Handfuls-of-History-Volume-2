Circa 1924

Punal is the Spanish word for dagger and has been associated with small knives of the Philippines also known as gunongs or punyals. They have a curved wooden handle, often ending with a knob, like the grip of a flintlock pistol, and silver collar at the base. The blades are usually double edged, either wavy or straight. They are sometimes likened to a poor man's kris. They can be rather elegant or very plain. The first knife I identified as a punal was owned by an acquaintance who wanted to sell it on eBay. Once I gave it a name and an exotic origin it inflated its value in his mind. I think he asked $20 for it but it was really a piece of junk with a rusty blade, a loose cross guard, and split in the scabbard. It would have been a liability to use or carry. This is a rather nicer example, although still nothing terribly fancy or special. It is a good example of the type. It would be carried in a belt sash with the clip holding it in place. The blade appears to be laminated steel but not typical pamor.
