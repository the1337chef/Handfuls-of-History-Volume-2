Modern Wootz

As described in the essay by Dr. Steve Bloom, the process for manufacturing the original crucible steel known to the medieval Europeans as “Damascus” has been lost to Western smiths since the age of the Vikings. True Damascus, or “wootz,” has special properties that come from the special process of its formation. Two modern smiths who have recreated the process are Alfred Pendray and Ric Furrer. Some years ago I contacted Ric and had him put me on his waiting list. About a year later he sent me these two pieces to choose from. I was happy to purchase them both.

The overall length on the smaller is 7.25 inches, with the metal blade (from the ivory to the tip) being 4.375 inches. He tempered this one to have a sort of hamon. The other has a walrus handle and is 8.25 inches overall, with a 5.25-inch blade including the integral guard.
