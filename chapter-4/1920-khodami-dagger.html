Circa 1920

This type of knife is associated with the Berber Kabyle tribes of southern Algeria who used the Flyssa-type swords as their primary weapons. This style of knife is also common in Morocco. It was likely made in Bou Saadi (from the Arabic, Bū Saʿāda, place of happiness), an oasis town in northeastern Algeria. The steel bolster and wood grip are bound with steel and twisted brass. Wood scabbard is covered with leather and also bound with twisted brass wires. There is a hole in the handle so that a leather cord could be passed through to serve as a lanyard.

It is a simple but serviceable knife, plain but well made. The blade has what are likely pictographic maker's marks. While the Flyssa is an impressive, intimating if not perhaps the most effective of swords, a Khodami is a knife far more likely to be used on a daily basis.
