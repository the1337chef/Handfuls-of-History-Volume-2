Circa 1860

The term Khanjar, simply translates as "dagger," and is applied to Turkish, Persian, and Indian weapons that do not fit a more specific category, such as a jambiya, or any of the various Indian types such as the chillanum or khanjarli. This dagger is from the later Ottoman Empire but probably not from Anatolia proper. The most likely attribution would be to an Ottoman subject of modern Syria or possibly Iraq. It is a very nicely made dagger, although more utilitarian than decorative. The handle is wood and bone and the blade is from low relief wootz steel. The scabbard is sturdy and well fitting, made of brass over wood. The front side is lightly incised with simple decorations, lacking any maker's marks or other inscriptions. The blade may be of Persian origin.
