Circa 1880

This type of knife is associated with the Mahsud, one of the Pashtun tribes living in the Khyber pass area between Pakistan and Afghanistan. It is basically a smaller version of the Khyber swords of this area. It shows Persian influence but is relatively plain in design. The 6.5-inch blade is forged from low-contrast Wootz (watered Damascus) steel. This knife is from the late 19th century but the style is still common today. One picture shows this knife next to a cheaply made modern version brought back by an American participant in the current military fiasco in Afghanistan.

The Mahsud and the other Pashtun tribes of Afghanistan have vigorously defied invaders for centuries.
